Our Current System:

The Federal Reserve sets monetary policy and operates key electronic payment systems. They act as intermediary when banks transfer money between each other

Banks allow us to make valid exchanges:
  • without having physical possession of currency
  • without being in the same physical location as the payee
  • without knowing/trusting the payee
Banks also safekeep our money to reduce the risk of theft, loss, destruction, etc.

Crypto



Cryptocurrencies are designed to be an alternate electronic payment systems:
  • A network of computers validates transactions instead of a bank
  • Cryptography is used to authenticate instead of banks
  • Accounts, balances, and transactions are tracked on a ledger
Pros:
  • Money is not backed by government-fiat currency
  • Less time for transactions
  • Less fees to banks, credit card companies
  • Less private data to banks and payees
    • Level 1 Chains: BTC, ETH, SOL, DOT, AVAX, Terra, MATIC, ALGO, COSMOS, All computers running the app (called nodes) maintain a copy of the ledger. Every transaction is sent to all the nodes and is added to the blockchain. To keep a node from adding a fraudulent transaction, a complex math problem must be solved by a node before they can add a block into the ledger. The first node to solve the problem gets a reward in coins and is allowed to write the block into the ledger. (Proof of work) (ETH 1.0) An alternate way is picking a node randomly based on the amount of funds "staked" on a node. The more staked funds, the higher chance a node has of being selected to get a reward and forge a block. To prevent a node from getting to forge a majority of the blocks, at a certain point more staked funds actually decreases a node's chance of being selected. (Proof of stake) (ETH 2.0) Stablecoins are digital assets that are designed to maintain a stable value relative to a national currency or other reference assets. Today, stablecoins are primarily used in the United States to facilitate trading, lending, or borrowing of other digital assets, predominantly on or through digital asset trading platforms Stablecoins facilitate a large and growing volume of digital asset trading by allowing market participants to quickly convert volatile digital assets into a digital asset with more perceived stability, and vice versa; providing a digital asset with more perceived stability to transfer across platforms without the use of national currencies and reducing the need for traditional financial institutions; and serving as a source of collateral against which market participants can borrow to fund additional activity, sometimes using extremely high leverage. Market participants also use stablecoins to earn yield by transferring stablecoins into digital asset trading platforms, or by using stablecoins to serve as collateral for loans and margined transactions, in exchange for interest or returns. As evidence of the importance of stablecoins to the digital asset market, stablecoins are reportedly among the most highly traded assets as a percentage of total volume on several large venues that enable the trading of digital assets.

    Tokens are farmed in these liquidity pools, in exchange for providing liquidity to decentralized exchanges (DEXs). Liquidity mining occurs when a yield farming participant earns token rewards as additional compensation, and came to prominence after Compound started issuing the skyrocketing COMP, its governance token, to its platform users. Impermanent loss - Bob puts in 100 UST and 100 LUNA - LUNA moons - Bob gets back more UST than LUNA - Alice puts in 100 UST and 100 LUNA - LUNA tanks - Alice gets back more LUNA than UST Liquidity pools often feature two assets — and while one might be a stablecoin such as DAI, the other could be a more volatile cryptocurrency such as ETH. Let’s imagine that a provider needs to offer equal levels of liquidity in both DAI and ETH — but suddenly, the price of ETH goes up. This creates an irresistible opportunity for arbitrage, because the price of ETH in the liquidity pool now doesn’t reflect what’s going on in the real world. To ensure the ratio of DAI to ETH remains balanced, other traders will buy ETH at a discounted rate until there’s equilibrium again. After arbitrage, a liquidity provider may end up with a greater amount of DAI and slightly less ETH. Impairment loss assesses the current value of their assets against what they would be worth if left sitting pretty in an exchange.
